Safety & Security

We want you to enjoy J&R Bookclub and feel safe and relaxed when participating. It's important to review the information below in order to ensure that J&R remains the lovely community initiative it aims to be.

Online

Staying safe online involves being aware of potential threats and taking steps to protect yourself. Read our guide below to help keep yourself safe online.

In person

J&R is all about forming connections and friendships, so if you find that you connect well with your book buddy and would like to meet in person then we suggest coming along to one of our organised events to meet them. Once you have met at one of our events, if you would like to meet again on your own terms or outside of the organised meetups then we encourage you to follow the safety tips below.

Over the phone

A phone call is a lovely safe way to connect with your fellow reader from the comfort of your own home. There are very few safety concerns when having a good old chinwag about a book together, but it's still important that you review our safety information below just incase.

More info...

Online Safety

  • Use strong and unique passwords: Use a combination of letters, numbers, and special characters in your passwords, and avoid using the same password for multiple accounts.
  • Keep your software and devices up-to-date: This will help protect you from known security vulnerabilities.
  • Be careful what you click on: Avoid clicking on links or downloading attachments from unknown or untrusted sources.
  • Use a firewall: A firewall can help protect your computer from unauthorized access.
  • Use security software: Anti-virus and anti-malware software can help protect your devices from harmful software and viruses.

In person safety

  • Take it slow: Don't rush into meeting someone in person. Take the time to get to know them online first and make sure you feel comfortable before agreeing to meet.
  • Meet in a public place: Choose a public place, like a coffee shop or park, for your first meeting. Avoid meeting at your home or the person's home.
  • Let someone know: Tell a friend or family member about the meeting, including where and when you're going, and give them the person's contact information.
  • Drive yourself: If possible, drive yourself to the meeting so you have your own means of transportation in case you need to leave quickly.
  • Trust your instincts: If at any point you feel uncomfortable or uneasy, trust your instincts and leave the meeting.
  • Be aware of your surroundings: Keep an eye on your surroundings, and be aware of anyone who may be acting suspiciously.
  • Be cautious with personal information: Avoid sharing personal information, such as your home address or phone number, until you feel comfortable and have built a level of trust with the person.

Over the phone safety

  • Don't give out personal information such as your address or bank details.
  • Do not give anyone money. If someone asks you to do this, then please hang up the phone and report it to us as soon as possible.
  • Trust your instincts: If at any point you feel uncomfortable or uneasy, trust your instincts and end the phone call.
  • Be aware of your surroundings: Keep an eye on your surroundings, and be aware of anyone who may be acting suspiciously.
  • Your person will have registered a phone number with us and we will have taken steps to verify their identity. In order to ensure your safety as far as possible they should only receive and place calls from the number they have registered with us.

If you have a safety or security concern please do get in touch with us to report it as soon as possible so that we can deal with it accordingly. All members of J&R are identity verified and have been DBS checked, but it is still important to remain cautious until you have built up trust with your book fellow.
