You can select any of the books in our library! Or you can select any title outside of the library too. You will discuss this with your book buddy and decide on a title you are both happy with. The idea of Jean & Rose is to be open minded too though. You won't learn anything from another generation if you refuse to read all their suggested material! So be open minded and see how learning from one another opens your eyes.

Most people like to talk atleast once a week. This gives you time to read through sections of your book and think about the things you want to talk about. But ultimately it's up to the two of you. If you want to speak more often, or you are both super busy and want to speak more like once a month, that's ok too. If you find that you want to talk lots, but your book buddy is not as free as you, you can ask to be paired with a second person and have more regular discussions with them whilst also still having the opportunity to talk with your original person.

At the moment we have a deal with council libraries to run J&R for free! But this might not be forever, as more people join and we see higher demand for more book titles we might have to charge a membership fee. If it does come to this, be assured that we will make sure you get plenty of brilliant books titles for your membership fee!

Your safety is extremely important to us. First of all, it's important to have taken the time to read through our safety and security tips on the Safety&Security page. But if you find yourself with a concern please get in touch with us immediately in order to resolve it as quickly as possible. You can do this by emailing us or calling the number at the top of the page. Remember, no one should be asking for any financial benefits or calling from different phone numbers. We suggest that for the first instance of meeting in person you do so at one of our organised events.

We expect all members to behave with respect towards each other at all times.Jean&Rose was created to foster kindness and stronger connections, friendship and community. We can do this through the active participation of our members, so we encourage you to join in! We prohibit any form of bullying, harassment, or discrimination and encourage anyone who feels that they are experiencing this to raise their concerns with us either through email or phoning us. We will respond within 24 hours and have a grievance procedure in place for such situations.

As much as we want to create stronger community connections, not everyone can be your cup of tea. Sometimes we just don't click with each other and that's ok. If you find yourself in this situation please don't worry and let us know as soon as you can. We can then pair you with someone else. We are adept at social communication and will do so subtly!

First of all, login through the J&R website (you can find the login button at the top of every page). You'll then find that a "search library" button opens at the top left of the page. Put in your book title, or a genre and have a browse. Once you have decided which book you would like to read then click "add to kitbag" underneath the book. It will then appear in your "kitbag". Head to the kitbag and select to have the book sent, or collect from your local library. You do need to be registered with your local library in order to take books out from there.

Happy members are the reason we do what we do! It's lovely to hear how J&R has had a positive impact on our community up and down the UK. If you have any comments, questions or would like to join, do get in touch.
